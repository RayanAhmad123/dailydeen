Privacy Policy

Daily Deen (“Daily Islamic Wisdom”) · Last updated 16 June 2026

Daily Deen is a personal content tool operated by an individual creator (“we”, “the operator”) that generates short educational videos about Islamic history, language, and teachings and publishes them to the operator’s own social media accounts. This policy explains what data the tool handles and how.

1. Who this applies to

Daily Deen is a single-creator publishing tool. It does not have public users, accounts, or sign-ups. The only person who authenticates with it is the operator, using their own TikTok and Google/YouTube accounts.

2. What we collect

  • Authorization tokens — OAuth access and refresh tokens for the operator’s own TikTok and Google/YouTube accounts, obtained when the operator grants access. These let the tool upload videos on the operator’s behalf.
  • Basic account identifiers — e.g. the TikTok open_id and YouTube channel/video IDs, used to target uploads to the correct account and to read back performance metrics for the operator’s own videos.

We do not collect data from any other person, do not process third-party or user-generated content, and do not use cookies or web tracking.

3. How we use it

Authorization tokens and identifiers are used solely to (a) upload and publish the operator’s own finished videos to the operator’s own TikTok and YouTube accounts, and (b) read back view and retention metrics for those same videos to help the operator improve their own content. No other use.

4. Storage and sharing

Tokens are stored locally on the operator’s own computer and are never sold, shared, or transmitted to any party other than the platform that issued them (TikTok, Google/YouTube) during normal API use. To produce videos, the tool also sends generated text and prompts to service providers (ElevenLabs for voice synthesis and Replicate for image generation); no personal account data is sent to those providers.

5. Retention and deletion

Tokens are retained until they expire or are revoked. The operator can delete all stored data at any time by removing the local token files and revoking the app’s access in their TikTok and Google security settings.

6. Your controls

Because access is granted by the operator to their own accounts, the operator can revoke it at any time from the platform’s connected-apps settings, which immediately stops all further access.

7. Changes

We may update this policy; the “last updated” date above will change accordingly.
